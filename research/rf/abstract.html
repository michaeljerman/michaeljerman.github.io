Nonparametric Regression Discontinuity Estimation with Random Forest Kernels

Regression discontinuity is an increasingly common tool in the applied economics toolkit. However, estimation results are often sensitive to the choice of kernel and bandwidth around the discontinuity. I show that the kernel generated by a random forest algorithm provides more efficient estimation of regression discontinuities than traditional kernels and bandwidths. The random forest kernel is a data driven approach which simultaneously selects asymmetric kernels and bandwidths with minimal input from the researcher.
